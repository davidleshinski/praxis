The interactive map below displays the perimeters of 16,069 wildfires that have spread across California since 1950.
California has a long history of being engulfed in smoke and flames on the daily and that hasn't changed.
Whether the fires were started purposely by humans, on accident, or as the result of climate change, California and those that live in high risk areas are hurting.
Find a way that you can help California and those affected by the fires here, here, or here.

Click the arrows on the left to view the map legend. The fire perimeters are colored by the amount of acres they spread across. Each of the five color bins countain about 3,214 different fires that fit in their respected bin's range.

  • YEAR_: The decade the fire occcured

  • STATE: The state the fire occured in

  • FIRE_NAME: The name of the fire

  • ALARM_DATE: Date the fire was reported

  • CONT_DATE: Date the fire was contained

  • CAUSE: What caused the fire

  • GIS_ACRES: How many acres the fire spread across

Data Source: California State Geoportal
